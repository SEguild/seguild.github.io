🏦 The Great Vault — Weekly Reward Chest

A guaranteed piece of loot every week, rewarding consistent engagement across all content types.

📍 Where to Find It

The Great Vault is located inside the Bank building in Silvermoon City, to the left of the banker NPCs. Look for the Vault icon on the minimap. It becomes claimable after each weekly reset — Tuesdays (NA) / Wednesdays (EU).

⚙️ How It Works

Each week you fill the Vault by completing content. The more you do, the more item choices appear — up to 9 options across 3 categories. However you may only claim one item per week.

For Mythic+, each chest slot's iLvl is determined by the lowest key among the runs required for that slot. For example: slot 1 needs 1 run at +10 → rewards +10 loot. Slot 2 needs 4 runs — the lowest of those 4 keys sets the reward. Slot 3 needs 8 runs — the lowest of all 8 determines the iLvl.

🪙 Thalassian Token of Merit

Don't like any choices? Take Thalassian Tokens of Merit instead (2 / 4 / 6 tokens based on slots unlocked). Spend them at Vaultkeeper Elysa near the Vault for Dawncrests, sockets, gold, or a Spark of Radiance to boost a crafted piece.

🔨 Crafted Gear & Embellishments

Crafted via the Work Order system — place orders with Mar'nah in Silvermoon City (/way #2393 45.16 55.62). You may equip a maximum of 2 Embellishments at a time.

Crafted Item Level

Crafted gear can be created at up to 1 rank below the max of any given track, using that track's Dawncrest as a crafting reagent. A Spark of Radiance (from Token of Merit) can push it to max rank.

2 Embellishment Cap

You can only have 2 Embellished items equipped at any one time — whether pre-embellished pieces or items with optional reagents. Choose your 2 wisely based on your spec's power profile.

Forms of Embellishments

Pre-Embellished Gear — unique effects built into the item (like mini tier sets). Optional Reagents — attachable effects added during the crafting work order for any eligible piece.

Common embellishment picks per class for Season 1, sourced from Method.gg class guides. Always sim your character for final decisions — meta tuning can shift mid-season.

💡 Universal first-craft advice: For most specs, crafting a weapon first is the highest power gain — weapon item level scales harder than armor. Use your first Spark on a weapon with Darkmoon Sigil: Hunt unless your class guide says otherwise, then fill remaining embellishment slots with spec-specific options.
